About

I am a trade economist interested in globalisation, firm behaviour, innovation, and technological change. My research examines how firms and industries respond to changes in international integration. In particular, I am interested in how those changes shape firms’ decisions about innovation and technological upgrading. To study these questions, I combine empirical analysis with theoretical modelling.

Current Interests

In my current research, I study how globalization shapes firm strategy, innovation, and technological change. One part of this work looks at how access to global markets affects the behaviour of multiproduct firms, especially their incentives to integrate different products into broader systems. Another part looks at how offshoring and access to lower production costs affect firms’ innovation choices, and in particular whether this shifts them away from more novel and exploratory technologies. More broadly, I am interested in how changes in international integration shape firm adjustment and industrial change.
