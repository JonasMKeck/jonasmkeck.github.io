About

I am a trade economist interested in globalisation, firm behaviour, innovation, and technological change. My research examines how firms and industries respond to changes in international integration. In particular, I am interested in how those changes shape firms’ decisions about innovation and technological upgrading. To study these questions, I combine empirical analysis with theoretical modelling.

Current Interests

In my revent work I have been trying to understand how globalisation affects decisions to invest in innovation and technology. In one of my projects, my coauthor and I investigate the behaviour of multiproduct firms. We analyse an aspect of these firms that has thus far received only limited attention, the extent to which a firm can integrate parts of their product portfolio into systems. We uncover novel facts in the data and then use a theoretical model of multiproduct firms to explain how access to global markets influences the decision to invest in the integration of multiple products.

In another projcet I if the access to lower labout costs trough offshoring has an effect on the decision to adopt new technologies. Taking inspiration from an older literature on cycles of technological leadership, I find that in industries that were more able to benefit from improved access to offshore labour were less likely to file patents in technologies that were not used in their industry before. Their patenting also became more concentrated on older technologies in the years following the integration of the offshore labour markets.
